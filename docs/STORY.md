# BaseBorn

## The Story of a Universe Built from Ruins

The universe did not end in fire.

It ended in silence.

For thousands of years, civilization expanded across the stars using a substance known as **Ether**—a form of living cosmic energy found inside planets, ancient machines, and the hearts of asteroids.

Ether powered everything.

Ships crossed entire galaxies in moments. Cities floated between stars. Artificial worlds were built in empty space. Stations became larger than moons, and fleets moved like living empires across the darkness.

Humanity believed Ether had made it unstoppable.

It was wrong.

---

## The Ether Collapse

No one knows who caused the Collapse.

Some believe the ancient empires consumed too much Ether. Others claim a forgotten civilization awakened something hidden at the center of the universe. A few surviving records speak of an experiment called **Project Born**, designed to create a self-evolving station capable of rebuilding civilization from nothing.

Whatever the truth was, the result was the same.

Every Ether network in the universe failed at once.

Hyperdrives collapsed.

Planetary shields disappeared.

Stations lost power.

Automated weapons turned against their creators.

Entire fleets were left drifting between worlds.

The great civilizations vanished within days.

The stars remained—but the universe they had built around them became a graveyard.

---

## The Long Dark

Centuries passed.

The surviving stations slowly broke apart. Their reactors became unstable, their defenses became hostile, and their storage vaults were buried beneath layers of dead machinery.

Without centralized power, the remaining pilots formed small roaming groups. Some became scavengers. Some became raiders. Some searched for lost colonies.

Most disappeared.

But Ether itself did not die.

It changed.

Fragments of Ether began growing inside asteroids, wrecks, and abandoned machines. The closer these fragments were to the center of the universe, the more powerful they became.

Raw Ether appeared in the outer regions.

Refined and Charged Ether formed deeper inside unstable space.

Radiant, Primal, and Core Ether began appearing near the center—where the Collapse had first started.

The universe was no longer empty.

It was waiting to be rebuilt.

---

# The BaseBorn

You are one of the **BaseBorn**.

BaseBorn are pilots raised inside emergency survival capsules created during the final days of the old civilization.

Each capsule carried:

* one pilot
* one damaged starter ship
* fragments of ancient navigation data
* and a single command:

**Find a station. Restore it. Begin again.**

You awaken at the edge of an unknown universe.

Your ship is weak.

Your weapons are basic.

Your cargo is empty.

You have no base, no fleet, and no guarantee that anyone else is still alive.

But somewhere in the darkness, an abandoned station is transmitting a broken signal.

That station is your first chance to survive.

---

# Your First Journey

At the beginning, survival is simple.

Destroy asteroids.

Collect Ether.

Avoid enemies.

Stay alive long enough to find a broken station.

Your ship can carry Ether, but it cannot use it directly. Ether is unstable in its natural form. To become useful, it must be delivered to a station and converted into **Station Fuel**.

Station Fuel powers everything:

* ship repairs
* weapons
* shields
* engines
* station systems
* crafting
* defenses
* docking bays
* mothership construction
* Galactic Station formation

Every journey away from your base becomes a risk.

The farther you travel, the better the Ether becomes.

But the farther you travel, the greater the danger.

If your ship is destroyed, all the Ether in your cargo is dropped into space.

Other pilots may take it.

Enemies may surround it.

Or it may remain there, waiting for you to return.

---

# The Broken Station

When you find your first station, it is barely alive.

Its central reactor flickers.

Its storage systems are offline.

Its docking arms are damaged.

Its defense turrets are silent.

Its hull is filled with scars from battles no one remembers.

You claim it anyway.

The moment the station recognizes you, it becomes more than a structure.

It becomes your home.

Your workshop.

Your respawn point.

Your shipyard.

Your vault.

Your fortress.

And eventually, the heart of something much larger.

But restoring the station is not easy.

Its systems must be rebuilt in stages:

1. Reactor Core
2. Fuel Converter
3. Storage Systems
4. Ship Upgrade Bay
5. Crafting Systems
6. Defense Systems
7. Landing Pads
8. Command Core
9. Full Restoration

Every repaired system unlocks a new part of the game.

The station does not simply become stronger.

It changes according to the choices you make.

---

# Build Something That Is Yours

There is no single perfect ship in BaseBorn.

There is no single perfect station.

Every player creates something different.

You may build a fast ship with powerful engines and missile launchers.

Another player may create a slow fortress covered in armor, shield emitters, and repair drones.

A third pilot may build a technology-focused ship filled with drones, scanners, support systems, and utility modules.

Even if two pilots begin with the same ship frame, their final ships may look and behave completely differently.

Every major upgrade changes more than numbers.

It can change:

* the ship’s silhouette
* its weapons
* its thrusters
* its armor
* its shield effects
* its movement
* its damage behavior
* its role in the team
* the way other players react to it

Your ship is not selected from a list.

It is built from your decisions.

---

# The Four Paths

Most pilots begin with one of four base ship philosophies.

## Balanced

Balanced ships are flexible and adaptable.

They do not dominate one area, but they can become almost anything.

A Balanced pilot may evolve into:

* a command ship
* a mixed combat vessel
* a mobile support carrier
* or a versatile mothership

## Tank

Tank ships are heavy, durable, and difficult to destroy.

They can carry stronger armor, larger shields, and more defensive systems.

A Tank pilot may become:

* a shield fortress
* a ramming juggernaut
* a defensive carrier
* or a siege mothership

## Speed

Speed ships are built for movement.

They accelerate quickly, turn sharply, and can escape dangerous regions before enemies can respond.

A Speed pilot may become:

* a scout
* an interceptor
* a hyperdrive vanguard
* or a fast mobile mothership

## Tech

Tech ships specialize in advanced systems.

They can control more drones, carry additional modules, improve scanning, and use complex equipment combinations.

A Tech pilot may become:

* a drone commander
* a research vessel
* a support network
* or a technology mothership

These are only beginnings.

The final identity comes from the way you build.

---

# The World Is Not Safe

The universe is filled with more than asteroids.

Ancient automated fleets still defend dead empires.

Scavengers hunt isolated pilots.

Raiders search for stations filled with fuel.

Central guardians protect regions where Core Ether forms.

Some abandoned stations are traps.

Some asteroid fields contain unstable objects that explode when destroyed.

Some regions interfere with navigation and hyperdrive systems.

And some threats are attracted to power.

The stronger your station becomes, the easier it is to detect.

A weak station may be ignored.

A powerful station becomes a target.

Eventually, enemies will come for what you have built.

---

# Death Has a Cost

When your ship is destroyed, you do not simply return without consequence.

Your cargo is dropped.

Your level is reduced.

Your ship is damaged.

You must wait before respawning.

If your station is still alive, you return there.

If your station is destroyed, you lose far more.

Stored Ether is lost.

Station Fuel is lost.

Upgrades are lost.

Defenses are lost.

Your team loses its respawn point.

The entire structure that supported your progress disappears.

You must return to the darkness and begin again.

This is why the station matters.

It is not only a menu where upgrades are purchased.

It is the physical location of your progress.

Protecting it is one of the most important parts of the game.

---

# The Center of the Universe

The closer you travel to the center, the more the universe changes.

Asteroids become larger.

Ether becomes rarer and more efficient.

Enemies become stronger.

Navigation becomes unstable.

The rewards become enormous.

The outer universe contains mostly Raw and Refined Ether.

Deeper regions contain Charged and Radiant Ether.

Near the center, Primal and Core Ether can be found.

A small amount of high-quality Ether can produce more fuel than entire cargo holds of common resources.

But central space is not designed for beginners.

Large stations compete for control.

Powerful teams defend resource fields.

Ancient guardians patrol the region.

And something still exists at the center—something connected to the original Collapse.

---

# Becoming a Mothership

A pilot who reaches level 100 may transform a fully evolved ship into a **personal mothership**.

This is not a normal upgrade.

It is a permanent transformation.

The ship becomes larger, slower, more powerful, and capable of supporting other ships.

Its final form depends on how it was built.

A defensive ship may become a Shield Bastion.

A support ship may become a Repair Ark.

An attack ship may become an Assault Carrier.

A technology ship may become a Drone Nexus.

A speed ship may become a Hyperdrive Vanguard.

Your build history determines your future.

The mothership carries your identity forward.

Its shape, modules, weapons, strengths, and weaknesses all reflect the decisions you made before reaching level 100.

---

# The Galactic Space Station

No pilot can rebuild the universe alone.

A team may contain up to six players.

Each team member can eventually bring a personal mothership.

When two or more motherships connect, they begin forming something greater.

A **Galactic Space Station**.

The Galactic Space Station is a massive modular structure created from the team’s connected motherships.

It is not one fixed model.

Its design depends on:

* how many motherships join
* which mothership types are connected
* what modules they carry
* which formation the team chooses
* how the station is upgraded

Each new mothership physically expands the station.

One mothership forms a command core.

Two create a twin-core structure.

Three create a larger multi-node station.

Four form a Galactic Outpost.

Five form a Galactic Fortress.

Six create a complete Galactic Space Station.

The team leader controls the structure.

The remaining players can operate weapons, drones, support systems, defenses, and docked ships.

---

# Team Composition Changes Everything

Every mothership contributes different abilities.

## Support Motherships

They provide:

* healing
* repair drones
* faster regeneration
* emergency recovery
* allied support

## Tank Motherships

They provide:

* armor
* stronger shields
* force fields
* collision resistance
* structural protection

## Attack Motherships

They provide:

* gun turrets
* missile batteries
* offensive drones
* stronger targeting
* increased damage

## Technology Motherships

They provide:

* scanners
* drone capacity
* improved crafting
* better fuel efficiency
* utility systems

## Speed Motherships

They provide:

* improved movement
* faster turning
* more efficient hyperdrive
* stronger dash systems

Different combinations create different Galactic Stations.

A team with multiple Tank and Support motherships may create a Guardian Citadel.

A team filled with Attack motherships may create an Assault Nexus.

A combination of Tech, Support, and Balanced motherships may create a Sanctuary Network.

No two teams need to build the same final station.

---

# The Price of Power

The larger the station becomes, the more fuel it consumes.

Fuel is required for:

* movement
* upgrades
* shields
* weapons
* repair
* dash systems
* formation changes
* hyperdrive
* mothership connections
* emergency systems

A six-mothership Galactic Station is extremely powerful.

It is also extremely expensive to maintain.

A team that stops mining will eventually become vulnerable.

A team that spends all its fuel on weapons may have weak defenses.

A team that builds enormous shields may struggle to move.

A team that focuses only on mobility may be easy to destroy.

Every powerful choice creates a new weakness.

---

# The Final Conflict

As teams grow, they move toward the center.

There, the strongest Ether exists.

There, ancient stations still orbit the ruins of the first Ether civilization.

There, the oldest machines are waking up.

Some pilots believe the center contains the source of Ether.

Others believe it contains the remains of Project Born.

A few intercepted signals suggest something more dangerous:

The Collapse may not have been an accident.

The first Galactic Stations were not created to save civilization.

They were created to fight something.

And that something may still be waiting.

---

# What BaseBorn Is About

BaseBorn is a game about building something valuable in a universe designed to destroy it.

It is about leaving safety to gather resources.

It is about deciding whether to spend fuel on your ship, your defenses, your team, or your future.

It is about creating a ship that reflects how you play.

It is about watching that ship become a mothership.

It is about joining your mothership with your teammates and creating a Galactic Space Station that no other team could build in exactly the same way.

It is about risk.

Progress.

Loss.

Cooperation.

And rebuilding.
