# BaseBorn

## A universe built from ruins

The universe did not end in fire. It ended in silence.

For thousands of years, civilization expanded through the stars using Ether: a living cosmic energy found inside planets, machines, and asteroids. Ether moved fleets, powered cities, and made artificial worlds possible. Then every Ether network failed at once.

Hyperdrives collapsed. Shields disappeared. Stations went dark. Automated fleets turned against their creators. Within days, the civilizations that believed themselves permanent became wreckage.

Centuries later, Ether returned in a different form. It grew inside asteroids and abandoned machinery. Raw Ether appeared in the outer reaches. Refined, Charged, Radiant, Primal, and Core Ether formed progressively closer to the center—where the Collapse began and its oldest machines still wait.

## The BaseBorn

You awaken inside a Survey Pod built during the last days of the old universe.

The pod carries one pilot, a navigation fragment, a mining emitter, and a final instruction:

**Find what survived. Repair it. Begin again.**

A derelict spacecraft drifts nearby. Its hull is broken, its command cradle is empty, and its systems no longer recognize a crew. The pod cannot survive the inner sectors alone, but it can harvest enough Raw Ether to restore the wreck's starter hull.

This is where every new pilot begins.

Mine the nearby asteroids. Collect the fragments before another survivor does. Carry the Ether back to the wreck. When its hull can hold pressure again, land in the command cradle and join the pod to the spacecraft.

The pod does not disappear. It becomes the craft's living center.

## Ether and the journey inward

The farther you travel toward the center, the more valuable Ether becomes—and the more dangerous the universe becomes with it.

- Raw Ether is common in the protected outer belt.
- Refined and Charged Ether form deeper inside unstable regions.
- Radiant and Primal Ether draw experienced pilots toward contested space.
- Core Ether exists close to the origin of the Collapse.

Asteroids are not scenery. They have mass, momentum, durability, and different resistance to mining. A reckless collision can tear apart an unarmored pod. A reinforced craft can turn the same collision into a mining strategy, breaking rock through speed and armor while accepting controlled damage.

When an asteroid breaks, its Ether enters the shared world. Any nearby pilot may collect it. Destruction and scarcity are remembered, and the asteroid takes five minutes to reform.

## Building a spacecraft

Every recovered spacecraft begins from the same hull foundation. What grows above and within that foundation is decided by its pilot.

Core tuning changes the fundamentals: regeneration, hull strength, collision damage, projectile velocity, penetration, weapon output, reload, and movement. At major levels, the mounted weapon system evolves. A pilot may split fire through Twin weapons, reach across the battlefield as a Sniper, command drones, accelerate into a ramming build, or follow another specialized branch.

These choices are physical. New barrels, emitters, launchers, drone bays, armor, and thrusters should change the visible identity of the craft—not only numbers hidden in a menu.

The Survey Pod is the beginning. The recovered spacecraft is the platform. The final ship is the history of the decisions made between them.

## Other survivors

You are not the only BaseBorn pilot.

Every connected pilot enters the same public universe. Their shots, asteroid kills, Ether drops, score, and movement exist in the shared world. The outer belt offers room to recover. Deeper sectors allow conflict, ambush, cooperation, and competition for rarer resources.

Pilots may form teams, invite other survivors, share ownership, choose leaders, and regroup around an active spacecraft. A team can protect miners, escort fragile builds, contest the inner sectors, or hunt the pilots above them on the leaderboard.

No pilot is required to fight alone, but no alliance is guaranteed to last.

## Death and recovery

Destruction has a cost. Cargo in open space can be taken. Progress is pressured. A pilot must choose when to return by using the Respawn button rather than waiting through a forced timer.

If the team still controls an active spacecraft, the pilot returns nearby. Otherwise, another Survey Pod reaches the safe outer belt and the recovery begins again.

The universe does not pause while someone is gone. Asteroids reform, teams move, resources change hands, and another name reaches the leaderboard.

## The center

The oldest records describe Project Born: an attempt to create a self-evolving command structure capable of rebuilding civilization after total collapse.

Some believe the Survey Pods were part of it. Others believe the recovered spacecraft are keys designed to assemble into something larger. Fragments suggest that multiple advanced team craft may one day connect into a mobile station—each pilot contributing weapons, defenses, engines, drones, or support systems to one shared structure.

That system is not complete. Neither is the story of the Collapse.

At the center of the universe, something is still transmitting.

It may be the source of Ether.

It may be the remains of Project Born.

Or it may be the reason the old universe went silent.

## What BaseBorn is about

BaseBorn is about beginning with almost nothing and building something worth protecting.

It is about leaving safety for better resources. It is about choosing speed over armor, range over fire rate, drones over direct weapons, or cooperation over isolation. It is about a small survival pod becoming the heart of a spacecraft shaped by the pilot inside it.

Mine. Repair. Integrate. Evolve. Connect. Survive.

**You are BaseBorn.**
