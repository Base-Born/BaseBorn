## BaseBorn — Complete Upgrade System

Below is the full upgrade structure based on the current BaseBorn design. Some exact numerical values still need balancing, but the systems and progression paths are defined.

# 1. Ship Core Progression

Every player ship has **7 major evolution stages**.

Each ship level gives the player **one upgrade choice**, allowing ships to develop differently depending on playstyle.

The ship upgrades independently from the base station. The station provides crafting, modifications, repairs, and support, but it does not control the ship’s core level progression.

## Ship Evolution Stages

### Evolution 1 — Salvaged Hull

The player begins with a damaged, basic spacecraft.

Capabilities:

* Minimal cargo capacity
* One weapon slot
* Weak hull
* Weak shields
* Low engine speed
* No hyperdrive
* Limited energy output

Visual identity:

* Exposed wiring
* Damaged armor
* Uneven hull plates
* Weak engine exhaust
* Visible sparks and damage

### Evolution 2 — Reconstructed Vessel

The ship becomes structurally complete.

Unlocks:

* Additional cargo capacity
* Improved hull integrity
* First shield upgrade
* Second equipment slot
* Improved engine efficiency
* Basic customization modules

Visual changes:

* Repaired armor
* Cleaner hull silhouette
* Stronger engine glow
* Additional plating

### Evolution 3 — Combat-Ready Ship

The ship becomes capable of sustained combat.

Unlocks:

* Additional weapon slot
* Weapon specialization
* Improved shields
* Better energy regeneration
* Thruster upgrades
* Defensive utility modules

Visual changes:

* Weapon mounts appear physically
* Armor becomes heavier
* Thrusters become more visible
* Shield effects become stronger

### Evolution 4 — Specialized Vessel

Players begin committing to a defined build.

Possible directions:

* Tank
* Damage
* Speed
* Mining
* Cargo
* Support
* Energy
* Hybrid

Unlocks:

* Specialized passive bonuses
* Advanced equipment modules
* Improved weapon compatibility
* Expanded cargo systems
* Ship-class visual transformation

### Evolution 5 — Advanced Warship

The ship becomes a high-tier combat or industrial vessel.

Unlocks:

* Heavy weapon compatibility
* Advanced shields
* Additional utility slots
* Improved energy core
* Specialized armor
* High-grade thrusters
* Advanced mining systems

Visual changes:

* Larger hull
* More complex silhouette
* Heavy plating
* Larger weapon systems
* Stronger reactor effects

### Evolution 6 — Command Vessel

The ship gains fleet and team capabilities.

Unlocks:

* Team support modules
* Docking assistance
* Fleet buffs
* Improved radar
* Long-range targeting
* Advanced defense systems
* High-capacity reactor

Possible roles:

* Fleet commander
* Heavy carrier
* Artillery vessel
* Defensive cruiser
* Industrial command ship

### Evolution 7 — Mothership-Class Vessel

This is the highest ship evolution.

Requirements may include:

* Maximum ship level
* Advanced base station
* High-quality ether
* Major crafting components
* Team progression requirements

Unlocks:

* Hyperdrive
* Multiple docking positions
* Team transport
* Fleet-wide bonuses
* Heavy weapons
* Mothership modules
* Massive cargo capacity
* Advanced defensive systems

Only one mothership can be active per team.

Docked allied ships increase the mothership’s total combat strength.

Possible improvements:

Possible improvements:

# 3. Weapon Upgrades

Weapons are physical modules attached to ship slots.

Players can replace, craft, improve, and specialize weapons.

## Weapon Properties

Weapons can be upgraded through:

* Damage
* Fire rate
* Range
* Projectile speed
* Accuracy
* Energy cost
* Heat generation
* Critical-hit chance
* Armor penetration
* Shield damage
* Area damage
* Tracking ability

## Weapon Categories

### Ballistic Weapons

Strengths:

* Strong hull damage
* Reliable sustained fire
* Low energy usage

Weaknesses:

* Ammunition or reload limits
* Less effective against shields

### Energy Weapons

Strengths:

* Strong shield damage
* High accuracy
* No physical ammunition

Weaknesses:

* High energy usage
* Can overheat

### Missile Weapons

Strengths:

* High burst damage
* Tracking
* Long range

Weaknesses:

* Slow reload
* Vulnerable to defensive systems

### Mining Weapons

Strengths:

* Efficient resource extraction
* Increased ether yield
* Specialized asteroid damage

Weaknesses:

* Weak in normal combat unless upgraded

### Heavy Weapons

Unlocked by advanced ship evolutions.

Examples:

* Railguns
* Heavy plasma cannons
* Siege missiles
* Beam weapons
* Mothership artillery

# 6. Hyperdrive Upgrades

Hyperdrive is a late-game mobility system.

## Basic Hyperdrive Rules

* Uses ether as fuel
* Any ether quality can be converted into fuel
* Higher-quality ether provides more fuel
* Activation takes 10 uninterrupted seconds
* Taking damage cancels activation
* Players can warp to selected valid locations

## Hyperdrive Upgrade Paths

Possible improvements:

* Reduced activation time
* Reduced ether consumption
* Increased maximum range
* Faster cooldown
* Improved warp accuracy
* Team warp capability
* Emergency short-range warp
* Reduced interruption penalty
* Improved fuel conversion
* Protected charging

## Team Warp

A mothership can transport allied ships that are docked before activation.

The number of ships transported depends on:

* Docking slot upgrades
* Mothership evolution
* Station technology
* Team upgrades

# 7. Base Station Progression

Players begin by finding a destroyed or abandoned station in the outer PvP rings.

The station must be:

1. Discovered
2. Claimed
3. Repaired using ether
4. Activated
5. Expanded
6. Specialized
7. Defended

The station cannot move until it is completely repaired.

Moving the station requires a level-100 ship.

---

# 21. Respawn Upgrades

The station acts as the player’s respawn point.

Base rules:

* Respawning is player-controlled through an immediate Respawn button
* Respawning causes loss of 70% of ship levels
* If the station is destroyed, the player loses everything connected to that station

Possible upgrade improvements:

* Reduced level loss
* Preserved cargo percentage
* Emergency reserve ship
* Faster post-respawn repair
* Temporary respawn shield
* Team rescue system

These upgrades must remain expensive so death continues to matter.

# Recommended Upgrade Principle

Every upgrade should produce at least one of these outcomes:

1. A visible physical change
2. A meaningful gameplay change
3. A new ability
4. A new strategic option
5. A meaningful trade-off

Avoid upgrades that only provide tiny invisible bonuses such as “+1% damage” unless they are part of a larger milestone.

The goal is that two players at the same level can still have ships and stations that look, behave, and fight completely differently.
